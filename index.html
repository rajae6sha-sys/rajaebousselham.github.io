Selected Projects

Multi-Layer Adaptive Thermal Enclosure Optimization

Problem: Passive enclosures often underperform across diverse climates due to fixed thermal properties.

Approach:

  • Transient heat transfer modeling in COMSOL
  • Multi-objective optimization (energy use & thickness)
  • Evaluation across Fairbanks, Phoenix, and Baltimore

Key Results: Identified optimal configurations minimizing annual energy consumption with thickness plateaus beyond 0.5 m.

Skills: Heat transfer, multiphysics simulation, optimization, climate analysis

Bio-Inspired SS-PCM Coatings for Passive Thermal Regulation

Problem: Conventional PCM systems lack spectral selectivity and adaptability.

Approach:

  • Design of surface morphologies inspired by Saharan silver ant and Morpho butterfly
  • Optical property tuning for radiative heating and cooling

Key Results: Demonstrated tunable emissivity and improved passive thermal performance.

Skills: Thermo-optical modeling, bio-inspired design, materials engineering

Machine Learning Prediction of Energy Consumption & Thickness

Problem: Optimization-driven simulations are computationally expensive for personalized designs.

Approach:

  • Neural networks trained on optimization datasets (~1800 points)
  • Hybrid input features including material and geometric parameters

Key Results: Accurate prediction of annual energy consumption and optimal layer thickness.

Skills: Python, TensorFlow/Keras, regression, sensitivity analysis

AFM Characterization of Polymer Blends

Problem: Distinguishing phase morphology in PS–LDPE blends at the nanoscale.

Approach:

  • AFM phase contrast and force-curve analysis
  • Correlation of mechanical response with material composition

Key Results: Clear identification of PS and LDPE phases.

Skills: AFM, nanoscale characterization, experimental analysis
